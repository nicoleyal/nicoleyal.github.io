Assignment 1: Laser Cut Press Fit Construction Kit!

Step 1: Getting Started

The process began by absorbing a lot of new information and steps in class. It was very helpful to go to the Mill and watch classmates with more experience (yay Hannah!) using the laser cutter.
I got to witness some troubleshooting which was very helpful. I measured my cardboard and began brainstorming for the design.

Step 2: Friday Night at the Mill

I headed back to the Mill on Friday night and found a very peaceful setting to work on my project. The staff was super friendly and helpful.
I got out my calipers and measured my cardboard again to make the correct sized notches.

Step 3: Troubleshooting

After following the steps in the slides VERY closely, I hooked up to the laser cutter and hit the final print button and nothing happened.
I called a staff member over and she helped me discover that I had to hit the left corner placement in the Illustrator print dialog box. I was so happy that it was such a simple fix and then my project showed up on the printer!

Step 4: True Test of Quality

When I brought my pieces home, my five year old jumped right into building with them. They stuck together easily for her.
This seemed like a pretty good success metric.
